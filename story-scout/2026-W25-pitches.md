## Pitch 1 — The Missing Weaver

**Story Bible thread:** **Part I — Main Characters**
**Premise:** Pukkai notices that a prominent textile artist in Dharavi's bustling weaving district is mysteriously missing, leaving behind only her latest finished piece with an eerie pattern. As she tries to unravel the mystery, Putum's keen senses pick up on subtle clues that suggest something more sinister is at play. Pukkai must balance her curiosity with caution as she delves into the world of Mumbai's vibrant textile industry.

**Key scenes:**
- Pukkai visits the weaving district and notices a peculiar lack of activity, only to find Putum sniffing around the artist's unfinished work.
- Pukkai discovers an online thread where fellow weavers share rumors about the missing artist's last project, hinting at a sinister plot by a rival textile giant.
- As Pukkai interviews witnesses and gathers evidence, she realizes that the eerie pattern on the finished piece might be more than just a coincidence – it could hold the key to uncovering the truth.

**Mumbai location(s):** Dharavi's weaving district
**Why this fits the world:** This story explores the intricate social dynamics of Mumbai's textile industry and the tensions between tradition and innovation in 2127.
**Analogues referenced:** The work of Indian textiles and the cultural significance of weavers in traditional societies.

**Issue slot candidate:** Issue #2

## Pitch 2 — The Gated Garden of Colaba

**Story Bible thread:** Pukkai notices patterns adults miss, particularly in Dharavi and its transformation

**Premise:** When the city's newest vertical farm in Colaba, a self-sustaining ecosystem for urban farming, starts experiencing mysterious power outages, Pukkai observes that the farm's AI system seems to be reacting with unusual urgency. Putum detects strange patterns of movement around the farm at night and communicates them to Pukkai through her collar, drawing her attention to an abandoned, hidden entrance beneath one of the elevated walkways.

**Key scenes:**
- [Scene] — Pukkai explores the hidden entrance while Putum stays behind to watch over it.
- [Scene] — Pukkai discovers evidence of a long-abandoned underground research facility hidden within the farm's foundation walls, which seems connected to an environmental disaster from Mumbai's past.
- [Scene] — Pukkai and Dipu uncover clues that hint at a cover-up by the city government regarding toxic waste disposal in Dharavi during its transformation.

**Mumbai location(s):** Colaba, particularly the area surrounding the vertical farm

**Why this fits the world:** This mystery connects to the larger theme of the world having answers but Pukkai carrying questions. It touches on issues like environmental sustainability, government transparency, and personal responsibility in a way that feels grounded in Mumbai's real-world growth and transformation.

**Analogues referenced:** Urban agriculture initiatives, underground research facilities, environmental disasters, government cover-ups

**Issue slot candidate:** Issue #3

## Pitch 3 — The Hidden Archive of Marine Drive

**Story Bible thread:** #3 — **Part I — Main Characters**

**Premise:** Pukkai notices that the Marine Drive promenade, once a popular spot for both locals and tourists, is starting to show signs of wear. While her Thatha Dipu takes care of their daily routine, she starts to investigate the reason behind this unexpected change. The issue begins with Pukkai discovering an unusual gap in the bioluminescent lighting system that runs along Marine Drive — the same one her Thatha always insisted on taking her to.

**Key scenes:**
- Scene 1: Pukkai and Putum visit Marine Drive at night, observing a strange delay in the bioluminescent lights' activation. Pukkai's curiosity is piqued as she wonders why this has happened.
- Scene 2: While researching possible causes online, Pukkai discovers an obscure article hinting at a hidden archive beneath Marine Drive that contains sensitive information — data from Mumbai's early days when the city was rebuilding itself after the devastating floods and industrial disasters of the past century. The piece mentions "the forgotten era" as a crucial time period, but no further details are available.
- Scene 3: Putum leads Pukkai to an unobtrusive alleyway beneath Marine Drive where she finds an old wooden door with intricate carvings that seem to match the symbols of an ancient Mumbai script — a long-lost language Pukkai's Thatha used to tell her about. The door is locked, but Putum's collar senses her nervousness and communicates her plan to try and open it.

**Mumbai location(s):** Marine Drive, Bandra Kurla Complex (BKC), the alleyway beneath Marine Drive

**Why this fits the world:** This issue delves into Mumbai's history of resilience and reconstruction — themes deeply ingrained in Pukkai's family legacy. The story questions how a city that has mastered efficiency and growth still finds itself facing unresolved historical issues.

**Analogues referenced:** A study on how cities deal with their past as they progress, similar to the way some urban areas have reclaimed industrial spaces for cultural or commercial use.

**Issue slot candidate:** Issue #4
